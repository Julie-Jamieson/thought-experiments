## Run 02

Selfish Percentage: 50%  
Regeneration Rate: 0.1

Ticks Observed:
117

Collapse Tick:
117

Observations:
- Cooperative agents disappeared rapidly
- Selfish agents initially expanded successfully
- Resource depletion accelerated quickly under reduced regeneration
- Environmental recovery was insufficient to sustain the population
- Full system collapse occurred by tick 117
- All agents died

Preliminary Interpretation:
Reducing environmental regeneration from 0.5 to 0.1 fundamentally changed the outcome of the simulation.

Under higher regeneration conditions, selfish extraction produced a stable but depleted equilibrium. Under lower regeneration conditions, the same behavioural dynamics rapidly exhausted the environment and produced total collapse.

This suggests that long-term system stability within the model is highly dependent on the relationship between extraction pressure and environmental recovery capacity.

---

## Comparison to Run 01

Run 01:
- Regeneration Rate: 0.5
- No collapse observed after 228,004 ticks

Run 02:
- Regeneration Rate: 0.1
- Full collapse occurred at tick 117

The contrast between these runs indicates that environmental regeneration thresholds strongly influence whether selfish extraction remains sustainable or becomes systemically destructive.

---

## Limitations

This simulation is highly simplified and intended only as an exploratory agent-based experiment.

The model does not represent real-world ecological, economic, or intelligent systems.
