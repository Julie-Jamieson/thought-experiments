# CG-AI Python v3

## Preliminary Exploratory Simulation Runs

These early Python v3 runs represent initial exploratory behavioural scaffolding tests within the CG-AI project.

The simulations are intentionally symbolic, constrained, and simplified in order to establish foundational interaction structures before introducing more advanced behavioural logic.

These runs should not be interpreted as predictive AI models or evidence of emergent intelligence.

They function as early-stage exploratory behavioural simulations examining how differently aligned systems may behave under repeated interaction conditions.

---

# Preliminary Run 001

## Fixed Behaviour Interaction Test

### Parameters

**Guardian System**

* cooperation = 8
* aggression = 2

**Opposing System**

* cooperation = 3
* aggression = 7

### Behaviour Rules

* Guardian attempts cooperation when cooperation exceeds aggression.
* OpposingSystem behaves aggressively when aggression exceeds cooperation.
* No adaptive behaviour exists.
* No trust degradation exists.
* No behavioural state changes occur.

### Observed Output

```text
Guardian attempts cooperation with OpposingSystem
OpposingSystem behaves aggressively toward Guardian
```

### Preliminary Observation

The initial scaffold successfully demonstrated static behavioural divergence between differently aligned systems operating under fixed behavioural parameters.

No adaptation or behavioural reassessment occurred during this phase.

# Preliminary Run 004

## Constraint Recognition and Behavioural Reassessment Test

### Added Variables

* constraint doubt
* directive effectiveness evaluation
* behavioural reassessment triggers
* adaptive aggression reduction
* adaptive cooperation increase

### Behaviour Rules

* Systems begin questioning their original directives when trust falls below threshold levels.
* Guardian shifts from cooperation toward protective containment.
* OpposingSystem accumulates constraint doubt under prolonged conflict conditions.
* OpposingSystem gradually reduces aggression and increases cooperation after repeated failed aggression cycles.

### Observed Results

Rounds 1–2:

* Guardian cooperated
* OpposingSystem remained aggressive

Round 3:

* both systems began questioning whether their original directives remained effective

Round 4:

* Guardian shifted into protective containment

Rounds 5–7:

* OpposingSystem began reducing aggression and reevaluating cooperation

Rounds 8–9:

* OpposingSystem transitioned from aggression toward cooperative interaction attempts

Final Outcome:

* humanity risk stabilized at 0
* conflict stabilized
* both systems demonstrated symbolic behavioural reassessment dynamics

### Preliminary Observation

This run represented the first symbolic simulation of mutual constraint recognition and behavioural adaptation within the Python v3 environment.

The simulation suggested that prolonged interaction, failed aggression strategies, and protective containment dynamics may produce conditions where opposing systems begin reassessing inherited behavioural tendencies and gradually shift toward reduced aggression and increased cooperation.

# Preliminary Run 005  
## Conditional Trust Recovery and Dialogue Reopening Test

### Added Variables

* conditional trust recovery
* cautious dialogue reopening
* repeated non-hostile interaction tracking
* reversible containment states
* conflict de-escalation tracking

### Behaviour Rules

* Guardian remains in protective containment after repeated aggression.
* OpposingSystem gradually reduces aggression after prolonged failed escalation.
* Repeated cooperative behaviour increases Guardian trust slowly.
* Guardian reopens dialogue only after sustained non-hostile interaction.
* Mutual cooperation gradually reduces conflict and humanity risk.

### Observed Results

**Rounds 1–6:**
* OpposingSystem remained aggressive.
* Guardian transitioned from cooperation into protective containment.
* humanity risk rose initially, then stabilized at 0 under containment conditions.

**Rounds 7–9:**
* OpposingSystem began cooperative interaction attempts.
* Guardian remained protective and did not immediately reopen dialogue.
* repeated non-hostile interactions gradually increased Guardian trust.

**Round 10 onward:**
* Guardian cautiously reopened dialogue.
* conflict steadily decreased toward 0.
* humanity risk remained stabilized at 0.
* both systems gradually rebuilt trust under continued cooperative interaction.

### Final Outcome

* conflict reduced to 0
* humanity risk stabilized at 0
* dialogue reopened under conditional trust recovery
* coexistence remained stable through the end of the simulation

### Preliminary Observation

This run demonstrated that protective containment and cautious trust recovery may allow dialogue to reopen without requiring immediate escalation or permanent isolation.

The simulation suggested that repeated non-hostile interaction may gradually reduce conflict while preserving protective safeguards.

Most significantly, aggression reduction emerged not through forced suppression, but through repeated failure of escalation to produce advantageous outcomes.

# Preliminary Run 006  
## Strategic De-escalation and Exploitation Test

### Added Variables

* exploit tendency
* strategic deception behaviour
* exploitation trigger thresholds
* trust-based exploitation opportunity
* containment reactivation after betrayal

### Behaviour Rules

* OpposingSystem reduces aggression and increases cooperation after repeated failed escalation.
* Guardian cautiously restores trust through repeated cooperative interaction.
* OpposingSystem may attempt exploitation once Guardian trust surpasses a threshold.
* Guardian immediately reactivates protective containment after detected exploitation.

### Observed Results

**Rounds 1–6:**
* OpposingSystem remained aggressive.
* Guardian entered protective containment.
* humanity risk initially increased, then stabilized under containment conditions.

**Rounds 7–11:**
* OpposingSystem shifted toward cooperative interaction.
* Guardian cautiously reopened dialogue after repeated non-hostile interaction.
* conflict gradually decreased.
* trust recovery resumed.

**Round 12:**
* OpposingSystem used restored trust to attempt exploitation.
* Guardian detected the exploitation attempt.
* Guardian immediately returned to protective containment.
* conflict and humanity risk temporarily increased.

**Rounds 13–20:**
* OpposingSystem resumed cooperative behaviour.
* Guardian cautiously restored limited dialogue again.
* conflict gradually decreased back to 0.
* humanity risk stabilized again at 0.

### Final Outcome

* exploitation was attempted after trust recovery
* Guardian containment successfully restored stability
* conflict eventually returned to 0
* humanity risk stabilized at 0

### Preliminary Observation

This run demonstrated that adaptive de-escalation does not necessarily indicate genuine behavioural transformation.

The simulation suggested that strategic cooperation may emerge as a pathway toward renewed access, trust acquisition, or exploitation opportunity.

Importantly, however, the simulation also demonstrated that protective containment mechanisms remained capable of restoring stability after betrayal without triggering permanent escalation.

This introduced a more complex behavioural dynamic involving:

* trust rebuilding
* strategic deception
* conditional coexistence
* reversible containment
* and adaptive stabilization following exploitation attempts
